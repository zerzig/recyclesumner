The Sumner County Resource Authority does not accept hazardous waste except for used oil and antifreeze.

All residents of Sumner County can recycle and dispose of waste at the Sumner County Resource Authority. Some schools have dumpsters for newspaper and magazines.

The Tennessee Department of Environment & Conservation would like to reduce the amount of used oil and antifreeze brought to the Fall hazardous waste collection. Used oil and antifreeze can be taken to the following sites in Sumner County:

Recycling:

B-Green Disposal & Recycling provides weekly garbage pick up service with free recycling service on the same day. They also provide recycling only pick up service. B-Green Disposal & Recycling provides a wheeled garbage container and bag stand for recycling free of charge. Only glass bottles need to be separated from other materials. All other items can be mixed together (single-stream recycling).

  • Aluminum cans and foil
  • Cardboard (boxes need to be broken down)
  • Glass containers (must be separated)
  • Newspapers, Magazines, Paperboard, & Junk Mail
  • Plastic Containers (#1-7)
  • Steel and Tin Cans
